'' the new york times ; obituary 31 january 1982 , p . 36 holloway 's film career continued simultaneously with his stage work ; one example was the 1956 comedy jumping for joy . american audiences became familiar with his earlier film roles when the films began to be broadcast on television in the 1950s . in 1956 holloway created the role of alfred p . doolittle in the original broadway production of my fair lady . the librettist , alan jay lerner , remembered in his memoirs that holloway was his first choice for the role , even before it was written . lerner 's only concern was whether , after so long away from the musical stage , holloway still had his resonant singing voice . holloway reassured him over a lunch at claridge 's : lerner recalled , '' he put down his knife and fork , threw back his head and unleashed a strong baritone note that resounded through the dining room , drowned out the string quartet and sent a few dozen people off to the osteopath to have their necks untwisted .
