( william williamson , p . 120 ) starting in 1775 , condeskeag became the site of treaty negotiations by which the penobscot people were made to give up almost all their ancestral lands , a process complete by about 1820 , when maine became a state . the tribe was eventually left with only their main village on an island upriver from bangor , called '' indian old town '' by the settlers . eventually a white settlement taking the name old town was planted on the river bank opposite the penobscot village , which began to be called '' indian island .
