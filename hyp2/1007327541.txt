a chart can take a large variety of forms , however there are common features that provide the chart with its ability to extract meaning from data each axis will have a scale , denoted by periodic graduations and usually accompanied by numerical or categorical indications when the data appearing in a chart contains multiple variables , the chart may include a legend ( also known as a key ) a bar chart is a chart with rectangular bars with lengths proportional to the values that they represent the first known bar charts are usually attributed to nicole oresme , joseph priestley , and william playfair a polar area diagram , sometimes called a coxcomb chart , is an enhanced form of pie chart developed by florence nightingale a streamgraph , a stacked , curvilinear area graph displaced around a central axis a gapchart , a time series chart showing evolving gaps and equalities between series a kagi chart is a time-independent stock tracking chart that attempts to minimise noise , at the close of the period are plotted with a line chart an allele chart is a chart originating from the study of genetics to show the interaction of two data points in a grid while charts can be drawn by hand , computer software is often used to automatically produce a chart based on entered data
