yawl rig appears to have originated in the early 19th century yachts were rigged as yawls as early as 1814 john macgregor had his 1867-built rob roy designed as a yawl before '' yawl '' became the name of a rig , it was a hull type generally , a yawl is a double-ended , clinker built open boat , which can be worked under sail or oar most were operated from a beach or a small harbour , with the boat being hauled out of the water when not in use there are examples in both shetland and orkney , with type and local variations in design the shetland boats include foureens and sixareens - the names denote the number of oars they were designed to use other smaller shetland types were the yoal and the fair isle yole until the 19th century , most of these boats were built in norway and then transported to shetland disassembled , where they were put back together again the norfolk and suffolk beach yawls were probably the fastest open boats ever built the fastest type of beach yawl was used for taking pilots and passengers out to ships later yawls were built in deal after this date , the yawl was less commonly used
