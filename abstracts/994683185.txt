the musical bow ( bowstring or string bow , a subset of bar zithers ) is a simple string instrument used by a number of south african peoples , which is also found in the americas via slave trade . it consists of a flexible , usually wooden , stick 1.5 to 10 feet ( 0.5 to 3 m ) long , and strung end to end with a taut cord , usually metal . it can be played with the hands or a wooden stick or branch . it is uncertain if the musical bow developed from the hunting bow , though the san people of the kalahari desert do convert their hunting bows to musical use . types of bow include mouth-resonated string bow , ground bow , and gourd-resonated string bow . nzewi , meki and nzewi , odyke ( 2007 ) . a contemporary study of musical arts : informed by african indigenous knowledge systems , volume 1 : the root : foundation , p.108 . african minds .
