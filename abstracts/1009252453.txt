in politics ( and particularly in international politics ) , soft power is the ability to attract and co-opt , rather than coerce ( contrast hard power ) . in other words , soft power involves shaping the preferences of others through appeal and attraction . a defining feature of soft power is that it is non-coercive ; the currency of soft power includes culture , political values , and foreign policies . in 2012 joseph nye of harvard university explained that with soft power , '' the best propaganda is not propaganda '' , further explaining that during the information age , '' credibility is the scarcest resource '' . nye popularised the term in his 1990 book , bound to lead : the changing nature of american power . in this book he wrote : '' when one country gets other countries to want what it wants might be called co-optive or soft power in contrast with the hard or command power of ordering others to do what it wants '' . nye , joseph.bound to lead : the changing nature of american power ( london : basic books , 1990 ) . he further developed the concept in his 2004 book , soft power : the means to success in world politics .
