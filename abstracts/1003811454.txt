image : american platinum eagle 2008 proof rev.jpg thumb 1 troy ounce ( 1.097 avoirdupois ounces , 31.1 g ) coin example ( platinum eagle ) troy weight is a system of units of mass that originated in 15th-century england , and is primarily used in the precious metals industry . the troy weights are the grain , the pennyweight ( 24 grains ) , the troy ounce ( 20 pennyweights ) , and the troy pound ( 12 troy ounces ) . the troy grain is equal to the grain-unit of the avoirdupois , the troy ounce is heavier than the avoirdupois ounce , yet the troy pound is lighter than the avoirdupois pound .
