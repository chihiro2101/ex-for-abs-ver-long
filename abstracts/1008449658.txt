the afghan whigs are an american rock band from cincinnati , ohio , united states . originally active from 1986 to 2001 , they have since reformed . the group – with core members greg dulli ( vocals , rhythm guitar ) , rick mccollum ( lead guitar ) , and john curley ( bass ) – rose up around the grunge movement , evolving from a garage band in the vein of the replacements to incorporate more r & b and soul influences into their sound and image . after releasing their first album independently in 1988 , the band signed to the seattle-based label sub pop . they released their major-label debut and fourth album , gentlemen , in 1993 . despite the name , none of the members is of afghan descent . pitchfork described them as '' one of the few alt-bands to flourish on a major label '' in the 1990s . dulli frequently claimed in interviews that the band would never get back together following their dissolution in 2001 but the group reunited in 2012 .
