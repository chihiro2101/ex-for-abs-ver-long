our man in havana ( 1958 ) is a novel set in cuba by the british author graham greene . he makes fun of intelligence services , especially the british mi6 , and their willingness to believe reports from their local informants . the book predates the cuban missile crisis , but certain aspects of the plot , notably the role of missile installations , appear to anticipate the events of 1962 . it was adapted into a film of the same name in 1959 , directed by carol reed and starring alec guinness . in 1963 it was adapted into an opera by malcolm williamson , to a libretto by sidney gilliat , who had worked on the film . in 2007 , it was adapted into a play by clive francis , which has since toured the uk several times and been performed in various parts of the world .
