in 1613 , the town was marked on a famous map of the grand duchy of lithuania — magni ducatus lithuaniae , et regionum adiacentium exacta descriptio printed in amsterdam and financed by the lithuanian magnate mikalojus kristupas radvila našlaitėlis . the site became the subject of wider interest again in the middle of 19th century , when a romantic writer , feliks bernatowicz , depicted the area in his novel '' pojata , córka lizdejki '' ( '' pajauta , daughter of lizdeika '' , warsaw , 1826 ) . the hillforts were soon excavated by the tyszkiewicz brothers and then by władysław syrokomla ( 1859 ) . after world war ii , the excavation works were restarted by vilnius university in the 1979 , and then again by the lithuanian institute of history between 1980–1983 . the state cultural reserve of kernavė was created in 2003 .
