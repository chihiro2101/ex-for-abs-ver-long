the humber bridge , near kingston upon hull , east riding of yorkshire , england , is a single-span road suspension bridge , which opened to traffic on 24 june 1981 . when it opened , the bridge was the longest of its type in the world ; it was not surpassed until 1998 , with the completion of the akashi kaikyō bridge , and is now the eleventh-longest . the bridge spans the humber ( an estuary formed by the rivers trent and ouse ) , between barton-upon-humber on the south bank and hessle on the north bank , connecting the east riding of yorkshire with north lincolnshire . both sides of the bridge were in the non-metropolitan county of humberside until its dissolution in 1996 . the bridge can be seen for miles around and from as far as patrington in the east riding of yorkshire and out to sea miles off the coast . it is a listed building . by 2006 , the bridge carried an average of 120,000 vehicles per week . the toll was £3.00 each way for cars ( higher for commercial vehicles ) , which made it the most expensive toll crossing in the united kingdom . in april 2012 , the toll was halved to £1.50 each way after the uk government deferred £150 & nbsp ; million from the bridge 's outstanding debt .
