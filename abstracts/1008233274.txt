armillaria is a genus of fungi that includes the a . mellea species known as honey fungi that live on trees and woody shrubs . it includes about 10 species formerly categorized summarily as a . & nbsp ; mellea . armillarias are long-lived and form some of the largest organisms in the world . the largest known organism ( of the species armillaria ostoyae ) covers more than in oregon 's malheur national forest and is more than 2,400 years old . some species of armillaria display bioluminescence , resulting in foxfire . armillaria can be a destructive forest pathogen . it causes armillaria root rot ( see plant pathology section ) of forests , which distinguishes it from tricholoma , a mycorrhizal ( non-parasitic ) genus . because armillaria is a facultative saprophyte , it also feeds on dead plant material , allowing it to kill its host , unlike parasites that must moderate their growth to avoid host death . in the canadian prairies ( particularly manitoba ) , armillaria is referred to often as , meaning '' near the stump '' in ukrainian .
