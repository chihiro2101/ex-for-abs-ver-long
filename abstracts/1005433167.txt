jan karol chodkiewicz ( c . 1561 – 24 september 1621 ; , , was a military commander of the polish–lithuanian commonwealth army who was from 1601 field hetman of lithuania , and from 1605 grand hetman of lithuania , and was one of the most prominent noblemen and military commanders of the polish–lithuanian commonwealth of his era . his coat of arms was chodkiewicz , as was his family name . he played a major role , often as the top commander of the commonwealth forces , in the wallachian campaign of 1599–1600 , the polish–swedish war of 1600–11 , the polish–muscovite war of 1605–18 , and the polish–ottoman war of 1620–21 . his most famous victory was the battle of kircholm in 1605 , in which he dealt a major defeat to a swedish army three times the size of his own . he died on the front lines during the battle of chocim , in the besieged khotyn fortress , a few days before the ottomans gave up on the siege and agreed to negotiate .
