a marsh is a wetland that is dominated by herbaceous rather than woody plant species . keddy , p.a . 2010 . wetland ecology : principles and conservation ( 2nd edition ) . cambridge university press , cambridge , uk . 497 p marshes can often be found at the edges of lakes and streams , where they form a transition between the aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems . they are often dominated by grasses , rushes or reeds . if woody plants are present they tend to be low-growing shrubs , and then sometimes called carrs . this form of vegetation is what differentiates marshes from other types of wetland such as swamps , which are dominated by tree , and mires , which are wetlands that have accumulated deposits of acidic peat .
