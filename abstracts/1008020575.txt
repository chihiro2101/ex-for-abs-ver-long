in ancient rome , the vestals or vestal virgins ( latin : vestālēs , singular vestālis were priestesses of vesta , goddess of the hearth . the college of the vestals was regarded as fundamental to the continuance and security of rome . these individuals cultivated the sacred fire that was not allowed to go out . vestals were freed of the usual social obligations to marry and bear children and took a 30-year vow of chastity in order to devote themselves to the study and correct observance of state rituals that were forbidden to the colleges of male priests . for an extensive modern consideration of the vestals , see
