a passphrase is a sequence of words or other text used to control access to a computer system , program or data . a passphrase is similar to a password in usage , but is generally longer for added security . passphrases are often used to control both access to , and operation of , cryptographic programs and systems , especially those that derive an encryption key from a passphrase . the origin of the term is by analogy with password . the modern concept of passphrases is believed to have been invented by sigmund n . porter sigmund n . porter .
