economic geography is the subfield of human geography which studies economic activity . it can also be considered a subfield or method in economics . scroll to chapter-preview links . economic geography takes a variety of approaches to many different topics , including the location of industries , economies of agglomeration ( also known as '' linkages '' ) , transportation , international trade , development , real estate , gentrification , ethnic economies , gendered economies , core-periphery theory , the urban economics , the relationship between the environment and the economy ( tying into a long history of geographers studying culture-environment interaction ) , and globalization .
