taphonomy is the study of how organisms decay and become fossilized or preserved in the archaeological record . the term taphonomy ( from the greek taphos , τάφος meaning '' burial '' , and nomos , νόμος meaning '' law '' ) was introduced to paleontology in 1940 by soviet scientist ivan efremov to describe the study of the transition of remains , parts , or products of organisms from the biosphere to the lithosphere . martin , ronald e .
