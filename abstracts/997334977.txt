jean buridan ; latin : johannes buridanus ; – was an influential 14th century french philosophy . buridan was a teacher in the faculty of arts at the university of paris for his entire career , focusing in particular on logic and the works of aristotle . buridan sowed the seeds of the copernican revolution in europe . kuhn , t . the copernican revolution , 1958 , pp . 119–123 . he developed the concept of impetus , the first step toward the modern concept of inertia and an important development in the history of medieval science . his name is most familiar through the thought experiment known as buridan 's ass ; however , this thought experiment does not appear in his extant writings .
