chelation is a type of bonding of ions and molecules to metal ions . it involves the formation or presence of two or more separate coordinate bonds between a denticity ( multiple bonded ) ligand and a single central atom . iupac definition of chelation . latin chela , from greek , denotes a claw . these ligands are called chelants , chelators , chelating agents , or sequestering agents . they are usually organic compounds , but this is not a necessity , as in the case of zinc and its use as a maintenance therapy to prevent the absorption of copper in people with wilson 's disease . chelation is useful in applications such as providing nutritional supplements , in chelation therapy to remove toxic metals from the body , as contrast medium in mri , in manufacturing using homogeneous catalysts , in chemical water treatment to assist in the removal of metals , and in fertilizers .
