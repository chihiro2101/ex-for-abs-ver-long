signy island is a small subantarctic island in the south orkney islands of antarctica . it was named by the norway whaler petter sørlle ( 1884–1933 ) after his wife , signy therese . the island is about long and wide and rises to above sea level . much of it is permanently covered with ice . the average temperature range is to about in winter ( i.e . in july ) . the extremes extend to . it is separated from coronation island to the north by normanna strait , and from moe island to the southwest by fyr channel . on signy island , the british antarctic survey ( bas ) maintains the signy research station , a scientific station for research in biology . the base was opened on 18 march 1947 , on the site of an earlier whaling station that had existed there in the 1920s . the station was staffed year-round until 1996 ; since that year it has been occupied only from november to april . it houses 10 people .
