polysemy or ; from , , '' many '' and , , '' sign '' ) is the capacity for a word or phrase to have multiple meanings , usually related by contiguity of meaning within a semantic field . polysemy is thus distinct from homonymy—or homophone—which is an accidental similarity between two words ( such as bear the animal , and the verb to bear ) ; while homonymy is often a mere linguistic coincidence , polysemy is not . in deciding between polysemy or homonymy , it might be necessary to look at the history of the word to see if the two meanings are historically related . lexicography list polysemes under the same entry ; homonyms are defined separately .
