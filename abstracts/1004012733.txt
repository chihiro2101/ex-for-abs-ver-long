'' jacques de molai '' , catholic encyclopedia . though little is known of his actual life and deeds except for his last years as grand master , he is one of the best known templars . jacques de molay 's goal as grand master was to reform the order , and adjust it to the situation in the holy land during the waning days of the crusades . as european support for the crusades had dwindled , other forces were at work which sought to disband the order and claim the wealth of the templars as their own . king philip iv of france , deeply in debt to the templars , had molay and many other french templars arrested in 1307 and tortured into making false confessions . when molay later retracted his confession , philip had him execution by burning on an island in the river seine in front of notre-dame de paris in march , 1314 . a history of the inquisition of the middle ages vol . iii by henry charles lea , ny : hamper & bros , franklin sq . 1888 , p . 325 . not in copyright . both the sudden end of the centuries-old order of templars and the dramatic execution of its last leader turned molay into a legendary figure .
