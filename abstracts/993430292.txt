in computer security , a covert channel is a type of attack that creates a capability to transfer information objects between processes that are not supposed to be allowed to communicate by the computer security policy . the term , originated in 1973 by butler lampson , is defined as channels '' not intended for information transfer at all , such as the service program 's effect on system load , '' to distinguish it from legitimate channels that are subjected to access controls by compusec . lampson , b.w . , a note on the confinement problem . communications of the acm , oct.1973.16 ( 10 ) : p . & nbsp ; 613-615 .
