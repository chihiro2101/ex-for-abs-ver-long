a count palatine ( latin comes palatinus ) , also count of the palace or palsgrave ( from german pfalzgraf ) , was originally an official attached to a royal or imperial palace or household and later a nobleman of a rank above that of an ordinary count . the title originated in the late roman empire . in the middle ages especially and into modern times , it is associated with the holy roman empire . ( accessed july 31 , 2019 ) . the office , jurisdiction or territory of a count palatine was a county palatine or palatinate . in england , the forms earl palatine and palatine earldom are preferred .
