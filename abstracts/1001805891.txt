r.a.v . v . city of st . paul , 505 u.s . 377 ( 1992 ) , is a case of the united states supreme court in which the supreme court unanimously struck down st . paul 's bias-motivated crime ordinance and reversed the conviction of a teenager , referred to in court documents only as r.a.v . , for cross burning on the lawn of an african-american family for violating the first amendment 's protections for freedom of speech . .
