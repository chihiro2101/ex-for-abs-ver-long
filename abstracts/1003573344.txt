in computing , a symbolic link ( also symlink or soft link ) is a term for any file that contains a reference to another file or directory in the form of an absolute or relative path and that affects pathname resolution . pathname resolution , posix . symbolic links were already present by 1978 in minicomputer operating systems from dec and data general 's rdos . today they are supported by the posix operating system standard , most unix-like operating systems such as freebsd , linux , and macos . limited support also exists in windows operating systems such as windows vista , windows 7 and to some degree in windows 2000 and windows xp in the form of shortcut files .
